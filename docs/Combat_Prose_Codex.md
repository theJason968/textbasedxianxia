# Xianxia Combat Prose Codex: Power Tier Narration Scaling

This document serves as the master reference for narrating combat scenes, ensuring that the player's power progression is reflected in the prose. As the player ascends the cultivation ranks, the verbs, sensory details, and scale of their actions must evolve from grounded, gritty physical struggles to transcendent, elemental dominance.

---

## 1. Mortal Middle Stage

**The Vibe:** A highly trained martial artist. Still fundamentally human, subject to human limits, but beginning to touch something deeper.

**Qi Manifestation:** Internal and subtle. It feels like a warm current in the belly or a brief surge of adrenaline. It cannot be projected outward.

**Enemy Perception:** Mortals see you as a dangerous, elite fighter. You can be overwhelmed by numbers.

- **Movement (e.g., Pine Shadow Step):** Fast, but requires physical footing. Dust kicks up. Breath is heavy.
  - *Example:* "You pivot hard on your heel, kicking up a spray of dirt as you weave past the bandit's blade. Your muscles burn with the sudden exertion."
- **Offense (Striking/Weapons):** Hits land with heavy, visceral impact. Flesh bruises, bones crack.
  - *Example:* "Your fist connects with the boar's flank, the impact shuddering up your forearm. The beast grunts, bruised but unbroken."
- **Defense (e.g., Iron Body):** Relying on bracing, dodging, and physical toughness. Taking a hit still hurts significantly.
  - *Example:* "You cross your arms and grit your teeth as the bandit's club strikes. The wood splinters, leaving a deep, throbbing ache in your forearms."
- **Technique Execution:** Feels like a physical exertion. Requires conscious breathing.
  - *Example:* "Drawing upon Azure Cloud Breathing, you force your racing heart to steady, squeezing a trickle of Qi into your tired limbs."

---

## 2. Mortal Late Stage

**The Vibe:** Peak human perfection edging into the supernatural. Movements are perfectly optimized. The body is saturated with Qi, making it unnaturally dense and resilient.

**Qi Manifestation:** Qi flows like a rushing stream through the meridians. It begins to influence the immediate surface of the skin and weapons, though it doesn't leave the body. Weapons might emit a faint hum.

**Enemy Perception:** Normal mortals feel a primal unease around you. Regular bandits hesitate. You can carve through mortal mobs with sustained effort.

- **Movement (e.g., Pine Shadow Step):** Blindingly fast to the untrained eye. Footsteps are nearly silent, defying physics slightly.
  - *Example:* "You slide across the uneven ground like water over stone. The bandit blinks, and your blade is already at his throat before his swing follows through."
- **Offense (Striking/Weapons):** Strikes carry concussive force that transcends mere muscle. The air snaps.
  - *Example:* "Your palm strikes the Mist Wolf's ribcage. There is a sickening *crack* echoing louder than the impact itself, as kinetic force ripples through its organs."
- **Defense (e.g., Iron Body):** Weapons begin to glance off or fail to penetrate deeply. Flesh feels like cured leather or hardwood.
  - *Example:* "The rusty blade chops into your shoulder, but halts abruptly against your hardened muscles, jarring the attacker's wrists."
- **Technique Execution:** Fluid and instinctual. Techniques flow into one another.
  - *Example:* "With a sharp exhale, your Azure Cloud Breathing surges unprompted, flooding your veins with cool energy and flushing the fatigue from your muscles."

---

## 3. Mortal Peak Stage

**The Vibe:** The absolute boundary of mortality. Oppressive physical presence. The character is a coiled spring of spiritual energy waiting to explode.

**Qi Manifestation:** Qi is visibly dense. It distorts the air around the cultivator like heat shimmer. Intent (*Killing Intent*) carries physical weight.

**Enemy Perception:** Mortals are instinctively terrified; they will break and flee if not forced to fight. Beasts bare their teeth in defensive fear.

- **Movement (e.g., Void Step):** Moving leaves afterimages. The character seems to teleport short distances rather than walk.
  - *Example:* "You step into the void. The wind from your sudden absence rustles the grass, and you materialize behind the cultist, leaving only a fading shadow where you stood."
- **Offense (e.g., Wind Blade):** Strikes project slightly beyond the physical limb/weapon. Air pressure acts as a secondary weapon.
  - *Example:* "You swing your spirit-grade shortsword. Even though the metal misses by an inch, the sheer pressure of your Qi parts the bandit's armor, drawing a thin line of blood."
- **Defense (e.g., Iron Body):** Attacks from lower-stage enemies barely register. Minor attacks shatter against an invisible aura before touching skin.
  - *Example:* "The bandit's arrow strikes your chest and shatters into splinters. You don't even break your stride, the kinetic force absorbed entirely by your dense Qi."
- **Technique Execution:** Effortless and commanding. The environment begins to react to the techniques.
  - *Example:* "As you channel Wind Blade, the stagnant air of the Hollow begins to swirl around your fingers, eager to obey your command."

---

## 4. Foundation Establishment Stage

**The Vibe:** Transcendent. No longer mortal. The character has built a spiritual foundation and is now a true cultivator. Gravity, fatigue, and mortal limits are suggestions, not rules.

**Qi Manifestation:** True elemental projection. Qi solidifies into visible light, frost, fire, or gale-force winds. The character glows with an inner light when fighting.

**Enemy Perception:** Mortals worship or despair. Mortal enemies are treated as environmental hazards, not threats. Peer fights are explosive, terrain-altering clashes.

- **Movement:** Gliding, hovering, or stepping on the air itself. Total environmental dominance.
  - *Example:* "You do not run; you command the space between you and the enemy to vanish. Your feet barely graze the earth as you cross the courtyard in a single, silent heartbeat."
- **Offense (e.g., Thunder Current Strike):** Cataclysmic on a local scale. Boulders shatter, earth craters, elements scream.
  - *Example:* "You unleash the Thunder Current Strike. A blinding arc of azure lightning erupts from your palm, vaporizing the Bone Hound instantly and leaving a scorched, smoking trench in the cavern floor."
- **Defense:** A permanent, passive Qi barrier (True Essence Shield). Mortal weapons disintegrate upon contact.
  - *Example:* "The Black-Thread Cultist thrusts his spear. It strikes your passive Qi barrier with a dull *clank*, the wooden shaft bowing and violently snapping in two without ever touching your robes."
- **Technique Execution:** The cultivator is not using the elements; they are *dictating* to them.
  - *Example:* "With a mere thought, Azure Cloud Breathing draws all ambient spiritual energy from the grove. The surrounding plants visibly wilt as you siphon the vitality of the world into your Core."

## Cross-Tier Combat Narrations

To make the power gaps feel earned, use these guidelines when the player fights enemies of different tiers:

- **Dominant (Player is 1+ Tiers Higher):** Describe the player's boredom, effortless grace, or the enemy's desperation. Focus on how little the player has to move. For example: *Mortal Peak vs. Mortal Middle Bandit* -> "You casually swat the bandit's desperate lunges aside, sighing at the sluggishness of his movements."
- **Contested (Player and Enemy are Equal Tier):** Focus on the clash of Qi, the physical strain, and the tactical use of techniques. The environment should suffer collateral damage. For example: *Mortal Late vs. Mist Wolf* -> "Your blade meets the wolf's frost-coated claws. Sparks and ice shards explode in a shower of light as your equal forces grind against one another."
- **Struggling (Player is 1 Tier Lower):** Emphasize survival. The enemy's aura is suffocating. Dodging is desperate, and blocking causes internal injuries. For example: *Mortal Late vs. Mortal Peak Cultist* -> "The cultist's mere presence makes it hard to breathe. You throw yourself into the mud to avoid his palm strike, the shockwave of his miss tearing the bark off the tree behind you."

## Companion Section: Technique-Specific Prose Rules

Use the power-tier sections above to decide the allowed scale of a fight. Use this companion section to keep each technique's identity consistent. The same realm should not make every move sound identical; a defensive body method, a movement art, a breathing method, and a lightning strike need different verbs, textures, and emotional weight.

### General Technique Rules

- **Name the technique sparingly.** A technique name is strongest at activation, mastery moments, and decisive turns. In repeated combat rounds, describe the effect rather than repeating the title.
- **Match the technique to the body.** Mortal techniques should still involve breath, stance, tendons, pain, and timing. Higher-stage techniques can involve air pressure, intent, light, sound, and environmental response.
- **Let mastery change confidence.** Low mastery should feel deliberate and costly. High mastery should feel instinctive, cleaner, and more economical.
- **Do not let early qi leave the body.** Before Mortal Peak, qi may reinforce muscles, skin, weapons, senses, and meridians, but it should not become a free projectile unless the technique explicitly supports that at the current tier.
- **Use local texture.** Ground every technique in the scene: cave grit, wet leaves, broken planks, mountain wind, incense smoke, blood heat, frost vapor, or iron dust.

### Azure Cloud Breathing

**Core Identity:** Rhythm, recovery, internal circulation, discipline under pressure. It is not flashy. It makes the player harder to break because the breath keeps returning.

- **Mortal Middle:** Breath is counted, forced, and sometimes ragged. Qi is a thread of warmth or coolness drawn through tired limbs.
  - Example: "You catch the next breath before panic can steal it, pulling a thin current of qi through your aching ribs."
- **Mortal Late:** The breath answers faster. It smooths fatigue, steadies hands, and makes follow-up movements feel inevitable.
  - Example: "Azure Cloud Breathing settles into its cycle, cool and exact, and the tremor leaves your sword hand."
- **Mortal Peak:** The breath creates pressure. Nearby enemies feel the rhythm before they understand it, as if the air is being measured around them.
  - Example: "Your breathing deepens once. The courtyard seems to quiet around that sound, and the bandit's courage thins with it."
- **Foundation Establishment:** Ambient qi gathers cleanly. Orthodox Azure Cloud imagery should feel controlled: mist, cloud shadow, clear pressure, and ordered circulation.
  - Example: "Mist gathers at the edge of each breath, drawn into a calm spiral before sinking into your foundation."

### Pine Shadow Step

**Core Identity:** Footwork, misdirection, narrow escapes, speed built from timing rather than raw teleportation.

- **Mortal Middle:** Shoes scrape, dust lifts, knees strain. The player is fast but still needs leverage and footing.
  - Example: "You cut sideways on the ball of your foot, close enough for the blade wind to tug your sleeve."
- **Mortal Late:** Movement becomes quiet and hard to track. Enemies see the player a heartbeat late.
  - Example: "Your step slips through the gap in his guard before his eyes finish following the old you."
- **Mortal Peak:** Afterimages and displaced air become acceptable. The technique feels like absence used as a weapon.
  - Example: "Your shadow remains in front of him for half a breath; by the time it fades, your palm is already behind his ribs."
- **Foundation Establishment:** Pine Shadow can become spatial dominance, but keep it elegant rather than flashy unless paired with Void Step.
  - Example: "You cross the broken stones without seeming to choose a path, each step arriving where danger has already failed."

### Iron Body Method

**Core Identity:** Hardening, endurance, pain control, body-as-armor. It should never read like dodging; it is the decision to withstand.

- **Mortal Middle:** Blocking hurts. The player survives by bracing correctly and accepting bruises.
  - Example: "You lock your forearms together and take the club on bone and breath. Pain blooms, but your stance holds."
- **Mortal Late:** Weapons bite shallowly or jar away. Attackers feel the recoil.
  - Example: "The knife edge catches your shoulder and stops there, trembling against muscle packed hard with qi."
- **Mortal Peak:** Lesser attacks break against aura-dense flesh. The player may not need to acknowledge weak blows.
  - Example: "The arrow bursts against your chest in a scatter of reed and iron, unable to make your breath change."
- **Foundation Establishment:** The body and qi barrier are unified. Mortal-force attacks become noise, not danger.
  - Example: "The spearhead reaches the pale light over your robe and folds sideways with a soft metallic whine."

### Wind Blade

**Core Identity:** Cutting pressure, speed, breath, invisible edges, tactical reach. Wind Blade should feel sharp and clean, not explosive unless overpowered or clashing with equal force.

- **Mortal Middle:** If available this early, keep it internal: quicker cuts, better alignment, air disturbed by weapon speed rather than projected qi.
  - Example: "Your blade rides the breath out of your lungs, fast enough to make the candle flame bow."
- **Mortal Late:** The weapon hums; cuts land with pressure beyond muscle, but still need a blade, hand, or close range.
  - Example: "The sword hums through the final inch, and the bandit's sleeve splits before the steel fully arrives."
- **Mortal Peak:** Short-range projected edges are appropriate. Misses can still score shallow cuts through pressure.
  - Example: "The visible swing falls short, but the wind it carries opens a red line across his guard arm."
- **Foundation Establishment:** Wind becomes a commanded element. It may shear stone, split formations, or cage a peer opponent in cutting currents.
  - Example: "You turn your wrist, and the gale narrows into a crescent bright enough to carve sparks from the cavern wall."

### Void Step

**Core Identity:** Displacement, absence, silence, wrongness. Void Step should feel stranger than Pine Shadow Step, less martial and more spatial.

- **Mortal Middle:** Avoid using true Void Step at this tier unless it is an unstable glimpse or failed instinct. It should feel dangerous and incomplete.
  - Example: "For an instant the space beside you feels thin, but your body lacks the courage to enter it."
- **Mortal Late:** The player may achieve brief skips or uncanny dodges, but the cost should be visible: breath loss, vertigo, cold skin.
  - Example: "You step where the shadow folds, reappearing a pace aside with frost prickling along your fingers."
- **Mortal Peak:** Short-distance vanishing, afterimages, and enemy confusion are the main vocabulary.
  - Example: "You leave the moment behind like a shed cloak, and the cultist's strike passes through your fading outline."
- **Foundation Establishment:** Void Step can bend pursuit, ignore mundane barriers, or reposition inside a battlefield with frightening calm.
  - Example: "The distance refuses to belong to him. You step once, and the space between his guard and your hand is simply gone."

### Thunder Current Strike

**Core Identity:** Lightning, nerve shock, explosive impact, sudden illumination. It should feel decisive and dangerous, with recoil or environmental consequence at lower mastery.

- **Mortal Middle:** This should usually be inaccessible. If foreshadowed, describe only a static prickle, numbed fingers, or an instinct the player cannot yet express.
  - Example: "A static ache crawls through your knuckles, gone before you can shape it into anything useful."
- **Mortal Late:** If learned early through unusual means, keep it unstable and close-range: shocking contact, muscle seizure, scorched cloth.
  - Example: "Your palm lands and a thread of thunder snaps through the point of contact, leaving both your hands shaking."
- **Mortal Peak:** Lightning can leap a short distance, crack stone, and stun enemies through armor.
  - Example: "Azure lightning crawls over your arm and lashes forward, turning the cultist's next breath into a broken gasp."
- **Foundation Establishment:** This becomes true elemental punishment: arcs, thunderclaps, afterimages burned into sight, and terrain scars.
  - Example: "Thunder answers before your hand finishes moving. The strike splits the chamber white, and the floor smokes in its wake."

### Post-Combat Reflection Rules

- **Dominant victory:** Emphasize restraint, distance, or the unsettling ease of survival. The player should notice how little the fight demanded.
- **Contested victory:** Emphasize cost, tactical memory, damaged surroundings, and what the enemy forced the player to learn.
- **Struggling victory:** Emphasize breath, injury, disbelief, and the exact mistake that almost ended the player.
- **Dark choices:** If the player uses corruption, harvesting, defilement, or death-qi actions, shift the prose toward appetite, cold, silence, staining, or spiritual residue. Do not make it feel clean.
- **Merciful choices:** If the player spares or aids an enemy, show the practical risk and moral texture rather than making mercy feel automatically rewarded.

### Enemy Reaction Rules

- **Mortals read the body first:** stance, breath, speed, wounds, fear, and confidence.
- **Beasts read threat and qi pressure:** hackles, defensive posture, retreat instinct, territorial fury.
- **Spirits read resonance:** cold, silence, flickering forms, attraction to impurity or death qi.
- **Cultivators read foundations:** meridian stability, technique control, qi density, aura discipline, and killing intent.

### Vocabulary Guardrails

- **Use sparingly:** "air snaps," "qi floods," "pressure," "blur," "effortless," "thunder answers." These are useful, but they flatten if repeated.
- **Prefer specific sensation:** tendon strain, grit under heel, sleeve tug, iron taste, frost crawling under nails, robe hem lifting, candle flame bending, stone dust jumping.
- **Keep orthodox Azure Cloud clean:** mist, cloud shadow, cool breath, ordered circulation, clear pressure.
- **Reserve corrupt imagery for corrupt acts:** wilting plants, oily shadows, sour qi, hunger, stains, rot, choking sweetness.
